Kaié:ri Nikawerá:ke Indigenous Bridging Program (BComm)

Program structure
Duration: One year, two terms (fall, winter) for full-time studies*
Credits: 24 credits
*Note: Students who wish to study part-time can meet with the Program Lead to discuss their course selection each term.

You will be enrolled in the prerequisite courses needed to transition into a Bachelor of Commerce (BComm) program, including Math and Economic courses, one English course, and two non-business elective courses of your choosing. Unique to the bridging program is a year-long seminar course designed for Indigenous students, with the goal of addressing your academic and holistic needs in a safe setting. Most credits earned in the bridging program will count towards your Bachelor of Commerce degree.

Courses

BComm Bridging Option (24 credits) – 2 terms
First term - Fall:

KNBP 200 Kaié:ri Nikawerá:ke Indigenous Bridging Program Seminar 1 (1.50)
UNSS 200 Self-Management Strategies (1.50)
MATH 206 Algebra and Functions (3.00)
ENGL 210 Introduction to Essay Writing (3.00)
an elective course selected by the student with advising (3.00)
Second term - Winter:

KNBP 201 Kaié:ri Nikawerá:ke Indigenous Bridging Program Seminar 2 (1.50)
UNSS 201 Successful Study Strategies (1.50)
MATH 208 Fundamental Mathematics I (3.00)
ECON 203 Introduction to Macroeconomics (3.00)

Note: Upon successful completion of the courses in the bridging program, students should consult with an advisor to review the requirements for the Bachelor of Commerce program.
